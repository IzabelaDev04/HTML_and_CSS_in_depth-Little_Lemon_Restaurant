Cultures On The Plate

Greek

Greek cuisine is deeply rooted in tradition, with an emphasis on fresh, simple ingredients that reflect the Mediterranean lifestyle. Olive oil, fresh vegetables, herbs, and seafood are central elements, alongside staples like feta cheese and yogurt. Meals are often communal, bringing people together to share dishes like mezes, and there’s a strong focus on hospitality and celebration of life’s moments with family and friends.

Italian

Italian cuisine is known for its regional diversity and its focus on high-quality, fresh ingredients. Pasta, olive oil, cheese, and tomatoes are the backbone of many dishes, but the cuisine varies significantly from north to south. Italian culture highly values food as an essential part of social life, with meals often lasting for hours, especially during family gatherings. Food and wine are celebrated as a way to connect with others and savor life.

Turkish

Turkish cuisine is a rich fusion of Central Asian, Middle Eastern, and Mediterranean influences, offering a wide variety of flavors and textures. Grilled meats, rice, bulgur, and rich desserts like baklava are typical, alongside an abundance of meze dishes. Turkish culture revolves around food as a central part of hospitality, and meals are often accompanied by tea or coffee. Sharing food with family and friends is an important social tradition, and meal times are cherished moments for connection.

About Us

Based in Chicago, Illinois, Little Lemon is a family-owned Mediterranean restaurant, focused on traditional recipes served with a modern twist. The chefs draw inspiration from Italian, Greek, and Turkish culture and have a menu of 12–15 items that they rotate seasonally. The restaurant has a rustic and relaxed atmosphere with moderate prices, making it a popular place for a meal any time of the day.

Little Lemon is owned by two Italian brothers, Mario and Adrian, who moved to the United States to pursue their shared dream of owning a restaurant. To craft the menu, Mario relies on family recipes and his experience as a chef in Italy. Adrian does all the marketing for the restaurant and led the effort to expand the menu beyond classic Italian to incorporate additional cuisines from the Mediterranean region.
